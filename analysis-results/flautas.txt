**3. Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1.  **Chicken Broth (0:04):** In a medium/large pot, add 4 cups of water.
2.  **Seasoning (0:05):** Add ½ small onion, 2 bay leaves, ½ tsp oregano, 2 peeled garlic cloves, and 1 tsp of salt to the water.
3.  **Chicken (0:10):** Add 2 raw chicken breasts to the pot.
4.  **Simmer (0:11):** Cover and cook the chicken for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked through.
5.  **Shred Chicken (0:14):** Remove chicken from the pot and place on a cutting board. Allow to cool slightly then shred the chicken using your fingers, or two forks. Place in a bowl.
6.  **Salsa Verde (0:18):** To the remaining broth, add 3 serrano peppers, 2 tomatoes, and 5 tomatillos.
7.  **Boil Vegetables (0:21):** Boil until vegetables change color; about 5 minutes.
8.  **Reserve Broth (0:24):** Reserve 1 cup of broth
9.  **Blend Sauce (0:25):** Place cooked tomatoes, tomatillos, and serranos into blender. Add reserved broth to the blender and 2 tsp of salt.
10. **Blend (0:27):** Blend until smooth. Then pour blended salsa into a serving dish.
11. **Prepare Tortillas (0:31):** Wrap 20-25 tortillas in a paper towel and microwave for 1 minute.
12. **Assembly (0:36):** Take a tortilla and add shredded chicken into the tortilla. Roll tightly, keeping seam down (0:37).
13. **Prepare for Frying (0:42):** In a large skillet, add enough oil to lightly deep fry the flautas.
14. **Fry (0:44):** Once the oil is heated, carefully add the flautas, seam side down. Fry the flautas until they are a golden brown color, about 2-3 minutes per side.
15. **Prepare Cream Mixture (0:48):** Mix sour cream with the salsa. The quantities were not exact
16. **Plating (0:53):** Once the flautas are golden brown, plate them and top them with the sour cream/salsa mixture, queso cotija, shredded lettuce, and more salsa verde.

**8. Tips and Variations:**

*   **Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
    *   Overcooking the chicken (it will be dry).
    *   Not heating the tortillas enough (they will crack when rolling).
    *   Overcrowding the skillet when frying (it will lower the oil temperature).
    *   Not draining excess oil (flautas become greasy).
*   **Substitution Options:**
    *   Chicken can be substituted with shredded beef, pork, or potato.
    *   Use any type of Mexican cheese.
    *   Use flour tortillas.
*   **Storage Instructions:** Store leftover flautas in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Store salsa verde separately.
*   **Reheating Instructions:**
    *   **Oven:** Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes until heated through and crispy.
    *   **Air Fryer:** Air fry at 350°F (175°C) for 5-7 minutes until heated through and crispy.
    *   **Microwave:** Not recommended as they will lose their crispiness.
